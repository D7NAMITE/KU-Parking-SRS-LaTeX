\chapter*{Abstract}
\label{chap:abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{\nameref{chap:abstract}}

Finding a vacant parking spot is a persistent challenge in high-traffic areas like university campuses, often leading to wasted time and increased driver frustration. Traditional sensor-based parking management systems are often overlooked by institutions due to high installation and maintenance costs. KU Parking addresses this by proposing a cost-effective solution that utilizes existing surveillance camera infrastructure integrated with computer vision to detect vacant spaces.

This report presents the Proof of Concept (POC) for the system, which employs the YOLO (You Only Look Once) object detection model and Intersection over Union (IoU) analysis to monitor parking occupancy. During this feasibility phase, the system was tested using recorded campus video and internet footage to demonstrate the model's ability to accurately identify available spots in a simulated real-time environment. The architecture is designed for cloud-based deployment to ensure scalability and ease of installation. Initial results indicate that the system can provide reliable occupancy data, offering a viable, low-cost alternative to traditional hardware-intensive parking solutions while significantly improving the campus navigation experience.
