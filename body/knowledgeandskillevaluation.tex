\chapter{Knowledge and Skills Evaluation}
\label{chap:knowledge-skills-evaluation}
\raggedbottom

This chapter presents a comprehensive evaluation of the knowledge and skills developed throughout the KU Parking project. It compares the program targets with three complementary perspectives: an AI-based assessment, a student self-evaluation, and advisor feedback. By combining these viewpoints, the chapter aims to provide a more balanced understanding of both technical progress and professional growth. The evaluation is structured around five key competency areas: Software Development; Machine Learning, LLM \& Agentic AI Systems; Data Management and Knowledge Systems; Critical \& Creative Thinking; and Collaboration \& Communication. To support clear interpretation, radar charts are used to visualize the alignment and differences across these competency areas, followed by reflections that explain the reasons behind the observed results.
\flushbottom

\subsection{Reflection on the AI Evaluation}
\label{reflection-ai-evaluation}
For each competency area, our team reflected on the AI evaluation results as follows:
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.2em, topsep=0.2em, parsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt]
    \item Software Development: The AI assessed this area above the expected level, and we agree with this result. Based on evaluator feedback from the project defenses, we refactored the system into a more modular structure by separating the mobile application, web portal, and AI component, and we also introduced caching for vacancy status. These improvements directly strengthened scalability, maintainability, and system responsiveness.
    \item Machine Learning, LLM \& Agentic AI Systems: The AI evaluation matched the expected level. Our team designed and evaluated the AI component using clear model metrics (Precision, Recall, F1 score, mAP50, and mAP50-95), and we compared multiple training-data configurations before selecting the final model. This reflects a solid ability to apply machine learning workflows within project constraints.
    \item Data Management and Knowledge Systems: The AI evaluation matched the expected level, which aligns with our implementation progress. We established data handling mechanisms such as storing human-drawn parking boundaries for future retraining and using caching for frequently requested vacancy information. These practices demonstrate practical data management for both system performance and long-term model improvement.
    \item Critical \& Creative Thinking: The AI evaluation matched the expected level, and we consider this appropriate. Throughout development, we addressed technical trade-offs, such as balancing dataset diversity and model generalization, and adapted our design after receiving feedback from project defenses. These decisions required both analytical reasoning and creative problem-solving.
    \item Collaboration \& Communication: The AI evaluation matched the expected level and reflects our team process. We worked with clear role separation, maintained regular weekly synchronization, and communicated continuously to resolve blockers quickly. In addition, presenting the project during the SKE Project Expo helped us explain technical decisions clearly to advisors, faculty, and peers.
\end{itemize}

    \item Data Management and Knowledge Systems: SKE expected level is 2, AI evaluation is 2, student self-evaluation is 2
    \item Critical \& Creative Thinking: SKE expected level is 3, AI evaluation is 3, student self-evaluation is 3

\subsection{Reflection on Self-Evaluation Compared with AI Evaluation}
\label{reflection-self-evaluation-compared-ai-evaluation}

For each competency area, we reflected on the AI evaluation results and found that we agree with the AI assessment as follows:
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.2em, topsep=0.2em, parsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt]
    \item Software Development: We agree with the AI evaluation in this area, where the assessed level is above the expected target. This is consistent with our practical progress, including improved modularity across the mobile application, web portal, and AI component, as well as better responsiveness through caching.
    \item Machine Learning, LLM \& Agentic AI Systems: We agree with the AI evaluation at the expected level. This matches our development process, where we performed model training, metric-based performance assessment, and iterative dataset experimentation.
    \item Data Management and Knowledge Systems: We agree with the AI evaluation at the expected level and consider it appropriate. Our implementation supports this result through structured storage of human-drawn parking boundaries and efficient vacancy information retrieval.
    \item Critical \& Creative Thinking: We agree with the AI evaluation at the expected level. During development, we continuously applied analytical and creative thinking to handle technical trade-offs and iteratively improved the model based on our hypotheses.
    \item Collaboration \& Communication: We agree with the AI evaluation at the expected level. This reflects our teamwork practice, including clear task ownership, regular communication, and coordinated presentations during project milestones and the SKE Project Expo.
\end{itemize}
